Dead Poets Society

Directed by Peter Weir
Why it is my favorite

Dead Poets Society is more than just a movie to me; it is a profound exploration of the human spirit and the struggle against the rigid expectations of society. It resonates with my interest in philosophy, specifically the concept of Existentialism.

"Carpe diem. Seize the day, boys. Make your lives extraordinary."

The core message of Carpe Diem is what draws me in. It’s a reminder that our time is fleeting and that we must find our own voice. I deeply empathize with Neil Perry. His character represents the tragic extreme of someone who feels trapped by an inescapable destiny set by others.

Personal Connection

The scene where Neil takes his own life is hauntingly beautiful yet devastating. It shows the weight of a soul that refuses to live a life that isn't his own. I understand that feeling of being pushed to the edge—where the pressure to conform becomes so heavy that the only way to "seize the day" feels like an act of final rebellion.

This movie mirrors the internal battles I face. It speaks to the part of me that values truth and authenticity over a comfortable lie, even when that truth is painful or misunderstood by the world.
